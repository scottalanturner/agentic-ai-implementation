## Course Description

This course is about something I've been watching happen in real-time: AI agents are quietly moving from demos into actual workflows inside actual companies. Over six weeks, we'll learn how to design, build, evaluate, and responsibly deploy them. We'll start by spotting the moments in your own work where an agent would earn its keep, then open up the hood to understand how agents actually reason. From there, we'll build — grounding agents in your company's knowledge, giving them tools, giving them a voice, letting them drive a browser, and chaining them together into small workforces. We'll close with the two things most AI courses skip: how to know whether your agent is actually any good, and how to ship it without getting yourself fired.

No programming background is required. You'll leave with a working agent you built, a practical vocabulary for talking about this stuff at work, and — I hope — a much clearer sense of where AI fits in your career going forward.

## Student Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, you will be able to:

1. **Explain** the core concepts of agentic AI — including autonomous reasoning, tool use, and memory — and **distinguish** agent-based systems from traditional chatbots and rule-based automation in enterprise contexts.

2. **Evaluate** organizational use cases and industry platforms for agentic AI deployment, assessing business fit, expected value, risk factors, and appropriate scope for responsible implementation.

3. **Design** and **configure** functional AI agents using no-code industry platforms, applying prompt engineering, knowledge grounding through retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), and tool interoperability standards — including the Model Context Protocol (MCP) and agent-to-agent (A2A) communication frameworks — to integrate agents with enterprise systems and workflows.

4. **Apply** responsible AI design principles — including guardrail configuration, human-in-the-loop oversight, and security practices — to build accountable agents and mitigate risk in organizational deployments.

5. **Build** and **present** a working AI agent for a professional use case, articulating design decisions, governance considerations, and measurable performance outcomes to a business audience.
