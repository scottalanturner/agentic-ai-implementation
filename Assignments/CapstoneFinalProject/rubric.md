# Capstone Project — Bring Your Own Agent: Grading Rubric


| **Criteria** | **Novice** | **Competent** | **Proficient** |
|--------------|------------|---------------|----------------|
| **Tool discovery and novelty** | **Points: 4** — Tool was used or demonstrated in this course, or is not genuinely agentic; or the brief does not explain why it qualifies. | **Points: 7** — Tool is new to the course and plausibly agentic, but the brief's justification for novelty or for the choice is thin or generic. | **Points: 10** — Tool is clearly new to this course and genuinely agentic; the brief explains specifically why it qualifies and why it was chosen for this student's field. |
| **The build** | **Points: 8** — No working agent — screenshots only, or the build does not run; or the build has no clear connection to an industry. | **Points: 14** — A functioning agent relevant to the student's industry, but overscoped, fragile, or only partially working in the demo. | **Points: 20** — A real, functioning agent relevant to the student's (or target) industry; works in a live demo or through a working link; scoped sensibly rather than overreaching. |
| **Presentation and demo** | **Points: 10** — Runs well over or under time; unclear or unrehearsed; demo fails with no backup; concept ties barely mentioned; cannot answer questions about the system. | **Points: 18** — Mostly within 5–6 minutes; generally clear; demo runs or backup is used; all four concept ties touched but briefly or unevenly; answers some questions but stumbles on how the system works. | **Points: 25** — Within 5–6 minutes; clear and well-rehearsed; live demo runs or backup is used smoothly; all four concept ties touched with one explored in depth; shows the class something genuinely new; states honest limits aloud; answers questions about the system's design, behavior, and limits with confidence. |
| **Written brief** | **Points: 14** — Missing required sections; Agent Card skeletal or absent; evaluation lacks documented test runs; risk and governance generic; business case absent or unsupported. | **Points: 25** — All seven sections present, but one or more of the four concept ties is thin — e.g., evaluation has fewer than three runs, the risk section names threat classes without specific mitigations, or the business case is asserted rather than defended. | **Points: 35** — All seven sections present and specific; Agent Card complete (eight elements); evaluation uses the four dimensions with three or more documented test runs; risk and governance names the real threat classes with specific mitigations; the business case is defensible. |
| **Honest limits and reflection** | **Points: 4** — Claims everything works, or limits are vague and boilerplate; "what I would do next" is generic or missing. | **Points: 7** — Names some real limits, but reflection stays surface-level or only loosely tied to this build. | **Points: 10** — Names real failures and real boundaries; "what I would do next" is concrete and tied to this specific build, not generic. |
