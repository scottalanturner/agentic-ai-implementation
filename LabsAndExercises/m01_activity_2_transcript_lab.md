# The Transcript Lab: From Raw Conversation to Actionable Intelligence

---

## Why We're Doing This

AI doesn't just answer questions about documents you feed it. It can *transform* them — turning messy, unstructured human conversation into clean, actionable work products that used to require 30–60 minutes of manual effort to produce.

Meeting transcripts are one of the most abundant raw materials in any organization. And almost none of that value gets extracted. Today you're going to change that — and see, firsthand, what AI can actually do in about five minutes.

---

## The Setup

Instead of working from a pre-written transcript, you're going to generate your own — one rooted in your actual professional world. This makes the exercise more useful and the output more meaningful to you personally.

Follow the three steps below to build your transcript, then work through the rounds.

> **Instructor note:** The goal isn't a perfect summary — it's to see *how much intelligence is buried in raw talk* and how different prompts unlock different kinds of value.

---

## Step 1 — Pick Your Meeting Type

Choose the type of meeting that best fits your professional experience. If you've never worked in a formal office setting, pick the one that sounds most interesting or relevant to where you're headed.

1. Project kickoff — a new initiative is being launched; scope and ownership are still fuzzy
2. Quarterly business review — the team reviews what's working, what isn't, and what needs to change
3. Vendor/tool evaluation — the team is deciding whether to buy, build, or switch something
4. Process improvement — a broken or inefficient workflow is being redesigned
5. Incident debrief — something went wrong and the team is figuring out what happened and what to fix
6. Resource/budget planning — stakeholders are negotiating priorities, headcount, or spending
7. Client strategy session — stakeholders align on approach for a specific client or account
8. Other — describe briefly in the prompt below

---

## Step 2 — Generate Your Transcript

Open Claude or ChatGPT. Fill in the two bracketed items, then paste the entire prompt below exactly as written.

```
Generate a realistic meeting transcript for a professional in the [YOUR PROFESSION OR INDUSTRY] field.

Meeting type: [PASTE YOUR CHOICE FROM THE LIST ABOVE]

Requirements — follow all of these exactly:

PARTICIPANTS: Include between 4 and 6 attendees. Randomly choose the exact number. 
Assign each person a realistic name, title, and role appropriate to the field. 
The group must include: at least one senior decision-maker, at least one skeptic 
or cautious voice, and at least one subject matter expert.

LENGTH: Randomly choose a meeting length between 30 and 60 minutes. Reflect this 
in the depth and pace of the conversation.

STYLE: Write it as a real recorded conversation — natural and sometimes messy, 
with people interrupting, hedging, or talking past each other occasionally.

REQUIRED CONTENT — the transcript must include all of the following:

1. At least 3 action items, each assigned to a named person. At least one must 
   have a specific deadline.
2. At least 2 decisions that are clearly and explicitly made during the meeting 
   (not just discussed — actually decided).
3. At least 3 open questions that are raised but NOT resolved before the meeting ends.
4. At least 2 risks or concerns that are flagged by participants.
5. At least 2 moments of tension or disagreement — these can be polite, but the 
   pushback must be clear.
6. One participant who comes across as uncertain, skeptical, or a potential blocker.
7. At least 2 assumptions the team is making that have not been confirmed or validated.
8. At least one important topic that gets mentioned briefly but is deferred or 
   avoided rather than resolved.

FORMAT:
- Begin with a header showing: Meeting name, date, platform (e.g., Zoom), 
  and a list of attendees with their titles
- Format all dialogue as: **Name:** spoken words
- Do not add a summary at the end — let the meeting wrap up naturally

Use realistic terminology, realistic organizational tensions, and realistic 
constraints (budget, time, compliance, competing priorities) that someone 
in this field would actually encounter.
```

## Step 3 — Read Your Transcript Quickly

Once the AI generates your transcript, skim it. You don't need to absorb every detail — just get a feel for who's in the room and what they're dealing with. Then work through the three rounds below using the same chat window.

---

## Your Challenges

Open Claude or ChatGPT. Paste the transcript above. Then work through each round.

---

### Round 1 — Surface Extraction (5 minutes)

Start with this prompt:

```
I'm going to paste a meeting transcript. After you read it, please extract:

1. All action items — who owns each one and any deadline mentioned
2. All decisions that were made (not discussed — actually decided)
3. Open questions that were raised but NOT resolved in the meeting
4. Key risks or concerns that were flagged

Format each as a clean, labeled list. Be specific — use names from the transcript.
```

**What to notice:** How much of the meeting's "work" was invisible until you extracted it? How many action items did people walk out without knowing they owned?

---

### Round 2 — Hidden Intelligence (5 minutes)

Now ask:

```
Look deeper at this transcript. Tell me:

1. Where is there tension or disagreement — even if it was politely expressed?
2. Who seems most uncertain, skeptical, or at risk of becoming a blocker?
3. What assumptions is the team making that haven't been validated?
4. What was NOT said — topics that seem important but were avoided or deferred?
```

**What to notice:** This is the layer that never makes it into meeting notes. Does the AI surface anything that surprised you?

---

### Round 3 — Produce a Work Product (7 minutes)

Pick **one** of the following and ask the AI to generate it based solely on the transcript:

**Option A — Project Brief**
```
Based on this transcript, write a one-page project brief that includes:
- Problem being solved and why it matters
- Proposed solution (as currently understood)
- Scope (what's in, what's out or TBD)
- Key dependencies and risks
- Next steps with owners
Use direct quotes from the meeting where they add clarity.
```

**Option B — Stakeholder Intelligence Summary**
```
Based on this transcript, create a stakeholder profile for each person in the meeting. For each person include:
- Their role and apparent priorities
- Their concerns or reservations
- Their level of commitment/enthusiasm
- What they need to stay aligned
Write it as an internal briefing for a project manager walking in cold.
```

**Option C — Risk Register**
```
Based on this meeting, create a project risk register with the following columns:
Risk | Likelihood (High/Med/Low) | Impact (High/Med/Low) | Who Raised It | Suggested Mitigation
Pull every risk, concern, and uncertainty mentioned — including the ones mentioned only briefly.
```

## Debrief (Class Discussion)

1. **What surprised you most** about what the AI extracted — in Round 1, 2, or 3?
2. **What did it miss or get wrong?** Where would a human reader have caught something the AI didn't?
3. **How long would Round 3 have taken you manually?** Be honest.
4. **What does this tell you about how AI could fit into your actual workflow?** Is this "AI helps me" or "AI does this for me"?
