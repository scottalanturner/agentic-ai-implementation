# The Agentic AI Discovery Interview: Find Your Automation Opportunities

**Course:** ISYS 398U — Agentic AI Implementation
**Lesson:** 01 — Orientation and Foundations


---

## The Problem

You've just learned what agentic AI is — systems that don't just answer questions, but plan, take actions, use tools, and complete multi-step tasks autonomously. But it's easy for that to stay abstract.

The harder question is: *where does it actually apply to your work?*

---

## The Goal

Use an AI agent to interview **you** — uncovering specific places in your job where agentic AI could act on your behalf, not just assist you.

The distinction matters. You're not looking for "AI can summarize this for me." You're looking for "AI can *do* this for me — end to end."

---

## The Exercise

### Step 1: Open Claude (or ChatGPT) and paste this prompt

```
You are an agentic AI opportunity analyst. Your job is to interview me and
surface specific, realistic places where an AI agent — one that can plan,
take multi-step actions, use tools, and operate with minimal human input —
could genuinely work on my behalf.

After EVERY answer I give you, do TWO things:

1. Generate a table of 3–5 agentic AI opportunities based on what I just told you.
   Each opportunity should represent a task where an agent could act autonomously,
   not just assist. Use these columns:
   - Agent Task (what the agent would do, not just help with)
   - How It Works (the tools or steps the agent would use)
   - Sample Trigger Prompt (how I'd kick it off)
   - What "Done" Looks Like (the output or action it completes)

2. Ask me ONE sharp follow-up question to get more specific — about my workflows,
   the tools I use, the handoffs I deal with, or the decisions I make repeatedly.
   You can ask me to paste in a sample document, describe a process step-by-step,
   or share an example from last week.

The more specific I get, the better your ideas become.

Start by asking me: What's your role, and what's one recurring task in your work
that involves multiple steps, multiple tools, or multiple people?
```

---

### Step 2: Go deep

Answer the AI's questions honestly and specifically. The goal is **agentic tasks** — things an AI could complete on your behalf, not just speed up.

Push yourself to think about:

- Tasks that currently require you to bounce between tools (email → spreadsheet → Slack → calendar)
- Work that follows a predictable pattern but takes real time
- Decisions you make repeatedly that are based on looking something up or checking something
- Handoffs where information moves from one person or system to another

The more specific your answers, the more useful the output. Share real examples, real tool names, real process steps.

---

### Step 3: Generate your Agentic Opportunity Map

After working through several questions, ask the AI:

```
Based on everything I've shared, create a summary table of all the agentic AI
opportunities we've identified. Include columns for: Agent Task, How It Works,
Sample Trigger Prompt, What "Done" Looks Like, and Estimated Time Saved Per Week.
Also flag which two or three you'd recommend I explore first and why.
```

---

## Debrief (Class Discussion)

Be ready to share:

1. **What was your most surprising agentic opportunity?** Something you hadn't thought of before this exercise.
2. **What made it "agentic" rather than just AI-assisted?** What would the agent actually *do* rather than just suggest?
3. **What would need to be true** for that agent to actually work? (Access to tools? A trigger? Guardrails?)

---

## Why This Matters

The gap between "AI can help me with this" and "an agent can handle this for me" is exactly what this course is about. Today's exercise is a preview of the whole semester: identifying that gap in your own work, and then learning how to close it.

Keep your opportunity table. We'll come back to it.
