# AI Tool Use and Data Handling Policy

**Effective:** January 1, 2025  
**Review Date:** January 1, 2026  
**Owner:** Data Governance Committee  
**Applies to:** All employees, contractors, and third-party vendors with access to company systems

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes requirements for the appropriate use of generative AI tools in day-to-day work. The goal is to enable productivity while protecting company, client, and employee data from inadvertent exposure or misuse.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and vendors who use company-issued systems or access company data, regardless of work location.

**Excluded:** The company's internal AI product engineering team operates under a separate Engineering Security Policy. Where the two policies conflict, the Engineering Security Policy governs.

---

## 3. Approved Tools

The following tools are approved for work use. Only company-licensed accounts are permitted — personal accounts on any AI platform are prohibited for work-related tasks.

| Tool | License Type | Approved Data Classifications |
|------|-------------|-------------------------------|
| Microsoft Copilot | Company Microsoft 365 license | Public, Internal, Confidential |
| ChatGPT Enterprise | Company license | Public, Internal |
| Claude.ai Team | Company license | Public, Internal |

No other AI tools are approved without written authorization from the Data Governance Committee.

---

## 4. Data Classification and Permitted Use

All company data is classified into one of four levels. Employees must verify the classification of any data before using an AI tool.

| Level | Classification | Description | AI Use |
|-------|---------------|-------------|--------|
| 1 | **Public** | Information approved for public release | Permitted with any approved tool |
| 2 | **Internal** | General business information not for external use | Permitted with any approved tool |
| 3 | **Confidential** | Sensitive business data, strategy, financials, unreleased product information | Permitted with Microsoft Copilot only; requires written manager approval before use |
| 4 | **Restricted** | Personally identifiable information (PII), protected health information, legal matter files, payment card data | AI tools are prohibited. No exceptions. |

**Exception process for Level 4 data:** Employees who believe a specific business-critical use case requires AI assistance with Level 4 data must submit a written request to the Data Governance Committee at `datagovernance@company.com` no fewer than 10 business days before the anticipated need. Approved exceptions are valid for 90 days and apply only to the specific use case described in the request. Blanket exceptions are not granted.

---

## 5. Output Review Requirements

**External distribution:** Any AI-generated content shared externally — with clients, regulators, auditors, or the public — must be reviewed and approved by a qualified human employee before distribution. This includes client-facing reports, regulatory filings, press releases, proposals, and contract language.

**Internal use:** AI-generated drafts, summaries, and internal memos do not require formal review before internal circulation. However, employees remain personally responsible for the accuracy of any content they distribute, regardless of how it was produced.

---

## 6. Record-Keeping

Employees must retain records of any AI-assisted work product that is shared externally for a minimum of **three years**, consistent with the company's general document retention policy.

Required records include:
- The prompt or instruction given to the AI tool
- The AI-generated output (before any human edits)
- A description of any material modifications made before distribution

Records must be stored in the designated project folder in SharePoint, not in personal drives or email.

---

## 7. Prohibited Uses

The following uses are prohibited regardless of data classification:

- Generating content intended to deceive, mislead, or misrepresent facts
- Using AI tools to make employment-related decisions (hiring, termination, compensation, performance ratings) without documented human review and HR approval
- Submitting AI-generated work as original human authorship when the context requires it (client contracts specifying human deliverables, academic submissions, etc.)
- Using AI tools to access, process, or infer information beyond your authorized access level

---

## 8. Violations

| Occurrence | Consequence |
|------------|-------------|
| First violation | Documented warning and mandatory AI compliance retraining |
| Second violation | 30-day suspension of all AI tool access |
| Third violation | Potential termination; referral to Legal for data breach assessment |

**Important:** Any violation involving Level 4 (Restricted) data is treated as a potential data security incident, regardless of whether it is the employee's first offense. These incidents are escalated immediately to the Chief Information Security Officer and are subject to mandatory regulatory notification review.

---

## 9. Questions and Escalations

- **General questions:** IT Helpdesk or `datagovernance@company.com`
- **Exception requests:** Submit via the process described in Section 4
- **Urgent compliance concerns:** Contact the CISO directly with CC to your department head
